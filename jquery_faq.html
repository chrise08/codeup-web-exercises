National Parks FAQs

How old is the National Park System?
The Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916, but President Ulysses S. Grant signed an act on March 1, 1872 which established Yellowstone National Park as the nation's first national park.

How many areas are in the National Park System?
419 areas across every state covering more than 85 million acres

How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Permanent: ~20,000
Volunteers: +279,000 in 2019

What is the largest national park?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK: 13.2 million acres

What is the smallest national park?
Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA: 0.02 acres

Can I bring my pet with me to a national park?
Some national parks welcome pets - each park has it's own policy regarding pets.

Is it free to visit a national park?
Less than one-third of the national parks charge entrance fees.
